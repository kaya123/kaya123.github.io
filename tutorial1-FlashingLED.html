Tutorial 1 - Flashing LED

Welcome to your first tutorial!

You will need:

  • 1x Arduino
  • 1x breadboard
  • 1x LED
  • 1x 22Ohm resistor
  • 2x wire

  • Step 1

    Connect one wire to "GND", and one to any of the numbers 1-13,
    for this tutorial we will be using '6'

    What does this mean?

    The holes in which you can insert your wires are called "pins" and those with numbers on them give power output, the ones with a '-' in front of them can give output from 0-255, whereas those without give either full power or nothing, depending on how you program them.
    The "GND" pin stands for "ground" and is the end of your circut.

  • Step 1

    Wire up the 220Ohm resistor and LED as shown in the diagram

    What does this mean?

    The breadboard connects all your components and wires without requiring soldering,
    The second diagram on the right shows which holes are conected to which others.
    The resistor reduces the amount of power going to the LED so it doesn't burn out
    The long leg of the LED is called the cathode and connects to the power source through the resistor.
    The short leg is called the anode and connects to ground.
